How Can a Person Be Saved?

Have you ever wondered what the Bible says about salvation?

Many people believe different things about how someone can be saved. Some believe that being a good person is enough. Others believe that joining a church or doing good works will earn eternal life.

But what does the Bible actually say?

This guide will walk you through what the Bible teaches about salvation in a clear and simple way. Each step will show Bible verses and explanations so you can understand how a person can receive eternal life through Jesus Christ.

Introduction

Before we begin, take a moment to think about this important question.

If you were to die today, are you sure that you would go to heaven?

Many people hope they will go to heaven because they try to live a good life. Others believe that religion, good works, or church membership will help them reach heaven.

However, the Bible gives us a clear answer about salvation.

The Bible teaches that eternal life is not something we earn by our works. Instead, it is a gift that God offers through Jesus Christ.

Let us now look at what the Bible says step by step.

Step 1 – Admit That We Are Sinners

Romans 3:23 (KJV) For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God.

The Bible teaches that every person has sinned. Sin is anything we think, say, or do that goes against God's will.

This includes things like lying, hatred, pride, disobedience, or any action that breaks God's commands.

Even if we try to do good things, the Bible says that all people have sinned and have fallen short of God's perfect standard.

Imagine standing before a judge in a courtroom. Even if someone has done many good things in life, one crime still makes them guilty before the law. In the same way, even one sin makes us guilty before God.

Do you believe that you, like every other person, have sinned before God?

Step 2 – The Penalty of Sin

Romans 6:23 (KJV) For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.

The Bible teaches that sin has a penalty. The word “wages” means something that is earned.

Because of sin, the penalty is death. This includes spiritual separation from God.

God is holy and just, so sin cannot simply be ignored.

Think of it like a debt that must be paid. If someone owes a debt but cannot pay it, the debt still remains. In the same way, our sin creates a debt that we cannot pay ourselves.

Do you understand that sin has a penalty according to the Bible?

Step 3 – Jesus Paid the Price

Romans 5:8 (KJV) But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.

Even though we are sinners, God loves us. Because of His love, He sent Jesus Christ to take the punishment for our sins.

Jesus died on the cross to pay the penalty that we deserved.

Three days later, He rose again, proving that He has power over sin and death.

Imagine someone paying a debt for you that you could never afford to pay. Because that person paid the debt, you are now free. Jesus did this for us when He died on the cross.

Do you believe that Jesus died and rose again to pay for your sins?

Step 4 – Trust Christ Alone

Ephesians 2:8-9 (KJV) For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast.

Salvation is not something we earn through good works.

It is a gift from God that we receive by faith.

To trust Christ means to rely completely on Jesus and what He did on the cross for our salvation.

Are you willing to place your trust in Jesus Christ alone for your salvation?

Assurance of Salvation

1 John 5:13 (KJV) These things have I written unto you that believe on the name of the Son of God; that ye may know that ye have eternal life.

God wants believers to know that they have eternal life. When a person truly trusts in Jesus Christ, the Bible promises that they receive eternal life.

Your Decision

Now that you have seen what the Bible says about salvation, the question becomes personal.

Will you trust Jesus Christ as your Savior?

Salvation is a gift that God offers to you.

If you believe that you are a sinner, understand the penalty of sin, and believe that Jesus died and rose again to pay for your sins, you can place your faith in Him today.

A Simple Prayer of Faith

Prayer itself does not save a person. Salvation comes by trusting Jesus Christ. This prayer is simply an example of how someone may express their faith in Him.

Dear Lord Jesus, I know that I am a sinner and that I deserve the penalty of hell. But I believe that You died on the cross, were buried, and rose again for my sins. Please forgive me for all my sins. Right now I place my trust in You alone as my personal Savior. Thank You for saving my soul and giving me eternal life. Amen.

Common Questions

Is being a good person enough to be saved?
The Bible teaches that salvation is not earned by good works. Ephesians 2:8-9 explains that salvation is a gift from God received through faith.
Do I need to join a church to be saved?
Church attendance is important for spiritual growth, but salvation comes only through faith in Jesus Christ.
What if I still struggle with sin?
Christians continue to grow spiritually. The Christian life is a process of learning and growing closer to God.

What Should I Do Next?

If you have trusted Christ as your Savior, here are some important next steps for your spiritual growth.

  • Read the Bible daily.
  • Pray regularly and talk to God.
  • Attend a Bible-believing church.
  • Follow the Lord in baptism.
  • Continue growing in your faith.

God's Invitation

Revelation 3:20 (KJV) Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me.

Jesus invites everyone to come to Him. The message of salvation is open to all who are willing to believe.

John 3:16 (KJV) For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.
